# Cozy Mystery

**The contract:** a murder, yes — but the world remains *safe*. Read `mystery.md`: fair play binds
here in full. This file adds the tonal contract, which is just as binding.

## What readers came for

**Comfort.** This is the point, and writers from outside the genre consistently underestimate it. The
reader wants the puzzle, the village, the tea, the recurring cast, and the profound reassurance that
order will be restored. The murder is an *intrusion* into a world worth protecting — not a window
into a world that was always rotten.

## Non-negotiable conventions

**No on-page graphic violence.** The body is found; the killing is not depicted. Blood is minimal.
Cruelty is not lingered on.

**The amateur detective.** Baker, librarian, innkeeper, gardener. They solve it because they *know
everyone*, not because they have a badge or a gun.

**The community is the setting and the substance.** The village, the small town, the shop. Readers
return to this genre for the *place* as much as the puzzle — often more.

**Order is restored.** The killer is caught, the community heals, the world is safe again. This is
the genre's emotional promise and it is inviolable.

**No graphic sex, minimal profanity.** The register is warm and clean throughout.

**Threat to the detective must be mild.** The reader must never seriously fear for them. Dread
belongs to a different genre entirely.

## Beats readers wait for

- The body, discovered in a domestic and slightly absurd location.
- The official police, dismissive of the amateur — and wrong.
- The community's secrets surfacing, each one small, human, and forgivable except one.
- The gentle confrontation.
- The return to normal. Tea. **The last chapter is the reward.**

## Exhausted tropes

The cat that finds clues. The recipe at the back. The handsome sceptical detective who becomes a love
interest. The village with an implausible homicide rate.

**Again: in this genre these are features, not bugs.** The reader chose this book *because* it has
them.

## The most common failure

**Tonal breach.** A moment of genuine horror, real cruelty, or graphic violence — and the contract is
broken. It does not matter how well written it was. The reader came here to feel *safe*, and one page
of real darkness costs the writer the whole book.

The murder is a **puzzle**, not a **trauma**. Hold that line.
